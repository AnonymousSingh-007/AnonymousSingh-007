### 🔴 [EVELYN](https://github.com/AnonymousSingh-007/EVELYN) — Quantum Graph Phishing Infrastructure Detection
> *They change their names. They can't change their shape.*

Phishing campaigns cycle domains faster than any blocklist can keep up — but the **infrastructure topology behind them stays constant.** EVELYN runs a continuous-time quantum walk over a phishing-infrastructure hypergraph and extracts a topology fingerprint:

$$\varphi(G) = \left| \langle j \mid e^{-iHt} \mid k \rangle \right|^2$$

This fingerprint is **invariant to domain names** — it detects a campaign by its *shape*, not its strings. Zero-shot on topologies never seen in training.

**My contribution / why it matters**
- 🧩 Framed detection as a **graph-spectral** problem instead of a string/GNN classification problem — the reason it generalizes zero-shot.
- 🔒 Robust to the #1 evasion tactic in phishing (rapid domain rotation) *by construction*, not by retraining.
- 📈 Building the empirical case for provable advantage over GNNs on infrastructure-shape tasks.

`Python` · `Continuous-Time Quantum Walks` · `NetworkX` · `DBSCAN` · `Graph ML`

<br>

<!-- ─────────────  SWARN  ───────────── -->
### 🟡 [SWARN](https://github.com/AnonymousSingh-007/MARL-Research) — Communication-Robust MARL for Adversarial Swarms
> *Most MARL assumes perfect comms. Real drone swarms never get that.*

A **1,050-run benchmark** that exposes a structural blind spot: QMIX cannot perceive its own communication state, so under jamming it coordinates confidently on stale information. The fix is a **two-process architecture** — a frozen QMIX explorer feeding a KL-anchored Navigator that stays stable when comms degrade.

| Under chase jammer | Baseline QMIX | SWARN (Navigator + QMIX) |
|---|:---:|:---:|
| Detection rate | 42.5% | **55.5%** |
| Δ | — | **+13.0 pts** |

**My contribution / why it matters**
- 🎯 **Diagnosed** the failure — showed QMIX's blindness to comms state is structural, not a tuning issue.
- 🛠️ **Engineered** the frozen-explorer + KL-anchored-Navigator fix and validated it across the full S1–S12 config grid.
- ✅ **46 passing tests**, VRAM guards, checkpoint auto-resume, strict env-parameter uniformity across all baselines — reproducibility built in, not bolted on.
- 📊 Paper-grade figures + full IEEE TNNLS LaTeX skeleton generated.

`PyTorch` · `QMIX` · `MAPPO` · `MARL` · `CUDA`

<br>

<!-- ─────────────  MIMIC  ───────────── -->
### 🟢 [MIMIC](https://github.com/AnonymousSingh-007/M.I.M.I.C.) — Behavioral Evasion via Synthetic Mouse Dynamics
> *The model that fooled every bot detector it met.*

A hybrid **LSTM + DDPM** framework that synthesizes human-indistinguishable mouse-motion trajectories — an adversarial stress test for behavioral-biometric defenses.

| Metric | Result | Meaning |
|---|:---:|---|
| Jensen–Shannon Divergence | **0.1478** | Near-perfect match to the human motion distribution |
| Bot-detector evasion | **100%** | Beat every detector it was tested against |
| Custom dataset | **212,568** samples | Hand-collected behavioral corpus |

**My contribution / why it matters**
- 🔬 Combined **LSTM temporal structure** with **DDPM stochastic realism** — neither alone hits human-level JSD.
- 🧪 Built the **212k-sample** behavioral dataset from scratch — the backbone of the whole result.
- 🛡️ Reframed as **defense research**: a generator this good is a benchmark that forces the next generation of detectors to actually be robust.

`Python` · `PyTorch` · `DDPM` · `LSTM` · `Adversarial ML`
